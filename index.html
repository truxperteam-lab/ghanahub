⚖️ Ghanan Laws & Website Compliance
Key laws every Ghanan & website owner should know
Data Privacy

🔒 Ghana Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843)

  • Who it applies to: Any person/business processing personal data of Ghanaians
  • User rights: Access, correction, deletion, objection to processing
  • Website duties: Must register with DPC, have Privacy Policy, get consent before data collection, report breaches promptly
  • Regulator: Data Protection Commission (DPC) — dataprotection.gov.gh
  • Penalty: Up to GH₵60,000 or 2 years imprisonment for individuals
Cybercrime

🌐 Electronic Transactions Act 2008 & Cybersecurity Act 2020

  • Hosting false/misleading information online is an offence
  • Identity theft and impersonation: criminal offence under Act 812
  • Unauthorised access to computer systems: up to 10 years imprisonment
  • Cyberstalking and online harassment are criminal offences
  • Regulator: Cyber Security Authority (CSA) — cybersecurity.gov.gh
Website Requirements

📋 What Every Ghanan Website Must Have

  • ✅ Privacy Policy — explains data collection & use
  • ✅ Terms & Conditions — rules for using your site
  • ✅ Disclaimer — especially for financial or legal information
  • ✅ Contact details — physical address or email
  • ✅ If collecting payments: CBN-licensed gateway required (Paystack, Flutterwave)
Labour

👷 Labour Act — Key Points

  • Minimum wage: ₵70,000/month (2024)
  • Work hours: Max 8hrs/day, 40hrs/week
  • Overtime: 1.5x normal pay
  • Maternity leave: 12 weeks with full pay
  • Annual leave: Min 6–12 working days
Tax

🧾 Tax Laws — Key Points

  • VAT: 7.5% on goods & services
  • Company tax: 30% (large), 20% (medium), 0% (small businesses under ₵25M revenue)
  • PAYE: 7%–24% based on income bracket
  • Filing deadline: June 30 annually
  • Authority: FIRS — firs.gov.ng
