⚖️ Ghana Laws & Your Rights
Key laws every Ghanaian should know
Data Protection

🔒 Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843)

  • Right to access, correct and delete your personal data
  • Businesses must register with the Data Protection Commission (DPC)
  • Consent required before collecting personal data
  • Regulator: DPC — dataprotection.org.gh →
  • Penalty: Up to GH₵60,000 or 2 years imprisonment
Cybersecurity

🌐 Cybersecurity Act 2020 (Act 1038)

  • Online fraud, hacking and identity theft are criminal offences
  • Cyberstalking and online harassment carry prison sentences
  • Unauthorised access to computer systems: up to 10 years
  • Regulator: Cyber Security Authority — csa.gov.gh →
Labour

👷 Labour Act 2003 (Act 651)

  • National Daily Minimum Wage: GH₵18.15/day (2024)
  • Max 8hrs/day, 40hrs/week; overtime at 1.5×
  • Maternity leave: 12 weeks with full pay
  • Annual leave: minimum 15 working days
Tax

🧾 Tax Laws — Key Points

  • VAT: 15% + 2.5% NHIL + 1% GETFL = 18.5% effective
  • Corporate income tax: 25% standard rate
  • PAYE: progressive 0%–35% on personal income
  • Authority: GRA — gra.gov.gh →
Consumer

🛡️ Consumer Protection Act 2020 (Act 1052)

  • Right to accurate product information and fair pricing
  • Right to return defective goods within a reasonable time
  • Regulator: Ghana Standards Authority — gsa.gov.gh →

🎵 Ghana Music & Culture
Afrobeats, Highlife and Ghanaian culture highlights
Music

🎤 Ghana Music Scene

  • Afrobeats & Afropop: Sarkodie, Stonebwoy, Shatta Wale, KiDi, Kuami Eugene, King Promise
  • Highlife: Ghana's iconic genre — Amakye Dede, Daddy Lumba, Gyedu-Blay Ambolley
  • Dancehall: Stonebwoy leading West Africa on the global stage
  • Awards: Vodafone Ghana Music Awards (VGMA) — held annually in May, Accra
Culture

🌴 Ghanaian Culture Highlights

  • Kente cloth: UNESCO-listed hand-woven fabric, worn at celebrations & festivals
  • National dish: Fufu with light soup or groundnut soup
  • Festivals: Homowo (Ga), Odwira (Ashanti), Damba (Northern Ghana), Aboakyir (Winneba)
  • Independence Day: March 6 — Ghana became the first sub-Saharan African country independent in 1957
  • Twi: Most widely spoken local language, across Ashanti & Brong-Ahafo regions
Tourism

🏖️ Top Ghana Tourism Spots

  • 🏰 Cape Coast Castle & Elmina Castle — UNESCO slave heritage sites
  • 🐘 Mole National Park — elephants, antelopes, hippos in the wild
  • 🌊 Labadi Beach & Kokrobite Beach, Accra
  • 🌿 Kakum National Park — famous canopy walkway
  • 🎭 Kumasi — Ashanti cultural capital, Kejetia Market, Manhyia Palace
  • 🦅 Wli Waterfalls (Volta Region) — highest waterfall in West Africa
